Intro

Filmmaker. Activist. Interdisciplinarian.

Lem is an award-winning filmmaker who has written and directed for the last 10 years in the US, UK, China and Singapore. Over the years, his interviews with hundreds across the world about their life stories form the backbone of his cinematic approach: moving and inspiring films based on stories in the real world. For him, meaningful cinema has the ability to transform lives.

Currently, Lem has exclusive access to make two feature films about: 1) the founder of Asia's largest self-defence movement, and, 2) the scientist who seeks to send a man to space in his homemade spacecraft.

Lem studied Art & Architecture in the renowned Cooper Union in New York before pursuing Writing & Directing in London and Producing at UCLA. He has previously worked in the food, finance, consulting and fashion industries, and currently volunteers for the endeavour to send a man to space. He has lived in 10 countries across 4 continents.


S H A R E            

My Life Story.

An interdisciplinary approach to self-expression.

In the early 2000s, as a fine arts student, I chanced upon the film Hero. I was enthralled by the use of colour, dance, camera movement, music and stage design. It struck me that Film is the truly interdisciplinary medium I had been seeking to express myself. This is both a blessing and a curse. With that, my journey as a filmmaker begun.

S H A R E            

Meaninglessness.

How my film career took off with a crash.

Upon graduation, I was forced to abandon filmmaking to pull my weight in the family's languishing food business. As a traveling salesman in the US, I survived out of a suitcase, rental car & cheap motels.

I used to do well in school. Somehow, I thought that was supposed to translate into a fulfilling career. Yet in the following years, I bounced from one firm to the next. Drifting through life without a purpose.

Beyond money & recognition, I was searching for an Inspiring Story to be a part of.

Alas, I gave myself a 6 month break from the corporate machine. I considered returning to filmmaking, but my emotional reservoir was running on empty. I took to the road again. Only this time, I wanted to discover myself.

Absolute freedom guarantees absolute loneliness. 12 years & 10 countries affirmed this truth.

Inspiration.

Empathy.

Allow yourself to be authentically Moved by strangers and you gain their trust in a heartbeat.

A few years ago, I learnt about a hermit warrior who undergone a crazy transformation after the killing of his brother. He traveled the world to learn true self-defence. He became a teacher and his methods are controversial. Chye opened up to me about his tragic life story.

Not just the war stories that we love bragging about, but the repressed ones we're dying to get out. I don't simply connect deeply with strangers. The art of inhabiting my characters' minds evolved out of a necessity to combat loneliness.

Calling.

Through hundreds of these stories, I found my way Home - my calling as a filmmaker who transcends visuals & self-expression.

This year, I was introduced to yet another phenomenal story. In the annals of Singapore history, it is unimaginable for an individual from this tiny country state to make an attempt at space travel. No less in his homemade spacecraft. I’m proud to play a part in telling Marvyn's astronomical journey.


My vision as a filmmaker is creating cinema that move & inspire us in our pursuit of Meaning in the face of pragmatism.

Man's Pursuit of Meaning.
