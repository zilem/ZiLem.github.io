Bio

Multidisciplinarian. Nomad. Activist.

Originally from Singapore, Lem studied Art & Architecture in the renowned Cooper Union in New York before pursuing Writing & Directing in London and Producing at UCLA. He has filmed in UK, China and US where he shot his award-winning feature Prescient in Niagara Falls. He previously worked in strategy consulting & investment banking at Morgan Stanley, KPMG and a fashion startup. He has lived in 10 countries across 4 continents.

My Story.

A multidisciplinary approach to self-expression.

In the early 2000s, as a fine arts student, I chanced upon the film Hero. I was enthralled by the use of colour, dance, camera movement, music and stage design. It struck me that Film is the truly multidisciplinary medium I had been seeking to express myself. Hence, my journey as a filmmaker begun.

Meaninglessness.

How my film career took off with a crash.

Upon graduation, I was forced to abandon filmmaking to pull my weight in the family's languishing food business. As a traveling salesman in the US, I survived out of a suitcase, rental car & cheap motels. In the following years, I bounced from one firm to the next.

Beyond money & recognition, I was searching for an Inspiring Story to be a part of.

Alas, I gave myself a 6 month break from the corporate machine. I considered returning to filmmaking, but my emotional reservoir was running on empty. I took to the road again. It's been 12 years & 10 countries of loneliness since.

Inspiration.

Empathy.

Allow yourself to be authentically Moved by strangers and you gain their trust in a heartbeat.

In a garage-converted-cafe in the Khayelitsha Slum of South Africa, I got the owner to open up about his criminal past. Not just the war stories that we love bragging about, but the repressed ones we're dying to get out.

Connecting deeply with strangers, skipping the small talk was how I evolved out of my loneliness.

Calling.

Through hundreds of these stories, I found my way Home - my calling as a filmmaker who transcends visuals & self-expression.

Since 2016, I’ve been working on the epic journey of a single father who survived childhood abuse and racial discrimination, to found Asia's largest Self-Defence movement to protect and empower women.

This year, I began work on the outlandish tale of a renowned scientist who despite his fall from grace, aims to send the first Singaporean to Space, in his homemade spacecraft.


My vision as a filmmaker is creating cinema that move & inspire us in our pursuit of Meaning in the face of pragmatism.
