Bio

Multidisciplinarian. Nomad. Activist.

Originally from Singapore, Lem studied Art & Architecture in the renowned Cooper Union in New York before pursuing Writing & Directing in London and Producing at UCLA. He has filmed in UK, China and US where he shot his award-winning feature Prescient in Niagara Falls. In a past life, he worked in strategy consulting & investment banking in Morgan Stanley, KPMG and a fashion startup. Nomadic at heart, he has lived in 10 countries across 4 continents.

My Story.

How my film career took off with a crash.

In 2004, I left filmmaking to pull my weight in the family's languishing food business. As a traveling salesman in the US, I survived out of a suitcase, rental car & cheap motels. In the following years, I bounced from one firm to the next, as were my peers.

Beyond money & recognition, we were all searching for an Inspiring Story to be a part of.

Alas, I gave myself a 6 month break from the corporate machine. I considered returning to filmmaking, but my emotional reservoir was running on empty. I took to the road instead. It has been 12 years & 10 countries since.

Tribe.

Connect.

Allow yourself to be Moved by others and you gain their trust in a heartbeat.

In a garage-converted-cafe in the Khayelitsha Slum of South Africa, I got the owner to be open about his criminal past. Not just the war stories that we love bragging about, but the repressed ones we're dying to get out.

Calling.


Through these stories, I found my way Home. My calling as a filmmaker.

Since 2016, I’ve been working on the epic journey of a single father who survived childhood abuse and racial discrimination, to found Asia's largest Self-Defence movement to protect and empower women.

This year, I began work on the outlandish tale of a renowned scientist who despite his fall from grace, aims to send the first Singaporean to Space, in his homemade spacecraft.


My vision as a filmmaker is creating cinema that move & inspire us in our search for meaning in a pragmatic world.
