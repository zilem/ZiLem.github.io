OF MEEK AND MENACE

Is Martial Arts Self-Defence?

AFTER surviving childhood abuse and racial discrimination, Chye, a single father and a martial artist, encounters the unjust killing of his brother, prompting him to abandon his stellar corporate career.

He travels the world to learn true self-defence, fulfilling his life's calling of founding Asia’s largest realistic self-defence movement to protect and empower women.


S H A R E            

Synopsis

An epic journey of self-discovery and survival.

INGENU

LITTLE Chye huddles in a corner of his bed. Traumatized. He hears his older brother Moss whipped and punched by their father for hanging out with gangs. Just another day in the life of this low-income Singapore family during the 70s.

One day, little Chye returns home to find Moss taken away by police for heroin possession. Witnessing his father abuse his mother for negliglence, little Chye musters his courage. Stands up to his old man for the first time. In that moment, he learns to stand up for the weak.



IMMIGRANT

AT 20, Chye, enrols in the University of Western Australia as a psychology major; the start of his journey of self-discovery. Working hard labour jobs to pay the bills, Aussies pick on him for no reason other than being Asian. Broken knuckles and a near loss of an eye prompts him to learn martial arts to defend himself.



CONFORMIST

AFTER graduation, Chye becomes fortuitously employed as a high level corporate executive in Singapore. For once in his life, he is able to amass wealth and finds love. Four years into his marriage with Jenny, at the brink of a divorce, they decide to salvage the marriage with a child. Nicole is born. To Jenny’s dismay, Nicole becomes the centre of Chye’s life, prompting Jenny to desert them both once and for all.



ACTIVIST

QUITTING his corporate job, Chye becomes a full time Dad to shower Nicole with the love he never received as a child. Meanwhile, he puts his psychological training to use; counseling rape and sexual assault victims.

One day, he receives news that a street attack has left Moss in a coma. Grieving over Moss' plight, Chye discovers his calling. He travels around the world to learn true self-defence. Returns to Singapore to found Asia’s largest realistic self-defence movement to protect and empower women.



FATHER

EIGHT years go by. Chye’s self-defense academy prospers. A new challenge arises. Chye receives a letter from Jenny’s lawyer petitioning for custody of Nicole. When the subordinate courts rule that Nicole is to be assigned to her mother, the heartbreak of being deprived of the one person he can’t live without drives him to take matters into his own hands...
