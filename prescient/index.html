Director's Statement

Determinism & Moral Ambiguity.

DETERMINISM, the theme in the 1997 film Gattaca, a world in which our fates are pre-determined by our genes. In Prescient, I explore determinism via the main character’s moral choices.

Where most films ascribe supernatural abilities to characters that give them an indisputable advantage, I'm intrigued by the idea of a hero who is imprisoned by it. Hence, Theodore Meridian's psychical ability to perceive the future deaths becomes more of a curse than a blessing.

As a character drama, I seek to enhance the characters’ relationships via composition and color. The interpersonal relationships between characters are echoed in their spatial relationship to the architecture they inhabit. This leads to wide shots with minimal cuts. Hand-held close-ups are used mainly for improvised intimate and emotional moments.

As a filmmaker, I've always been drawn to the infallible character who makes imperfect choices whilst navigating within his morally ambiguous world. Prescient is the first of my attempts at understanding that world. Our world.
