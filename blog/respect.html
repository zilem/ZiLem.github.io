An Epiphany about Respect.

How I lost a big brother.

I HAVE NO siblings. When I was young, I grew up with a cousin three years older than me. He was practically my big brother. He behaved like one and demanded to be respected like one.

When I went overseas for my university and returned one summer, he noticed that I spoke with an open mind. To that he said, “I don’t like that you're better than me now that you've studied abroad.”

WHEN I STARTED working, I realised to my horror that I absolutely hated the corporate life. After two years, I quit and went freelance. Meanwhile, my cousin took to corporate life like a duck to water. At one point, he had several hundred workers under him. He absolutely loved that authority.

Then all of that plateaued. He began dreaming about side projects to run. Since I was a freelancer, I encouraged him to be entrepreneurial. But corporate life was too comfortable for him to take any action. So those dreams remained largely as dreams.

Once in a while, he contemplated business ventures that processed the by-products of his employer. Except he wouldn't declare his interest in them. Not only was it illegal, it was highly immoral. Thankfully his latent inertia got the better of him.


WHEN HE FINALLY took action, it was on a revolutionary product that didn’t involve his employer. I supported him wholeheartedly. I got him to consider launching it on Kickstarter and offered to make the marketing video. For free.

I shared many of my creative ideas. Because of his technical background, he didn’t appreciate any of them. All he wanted was for me to make a boring, technical, cookie-cutter video.

I procrastinated. For weeks. Finally, I confronted my own inaction. And it hit me like a lightning bolt.


I was seeking respect from him.

I HAVE BEEN seeking respect from him for the last thirty years. And no matter how hard I try, I’m never gonna get it. Because he’s never gonna give it to me. Not for this project. Not ever. Then I unearthed something even deeper.


I wanted him to take action so that I can finally respect him.

Realising this sent chills down my spine. Clearly, this relationship wasn’t going to work. Neither party respected the other. But why? Why had I been oblivious to this? For thirty years?

I questioned myself rigorously. I came up with a framework for evaluating deep collaborative relationships. We know it comes down to respect.

But what should we respect someone for?

WE ALL KNOW genuine respect is not earned through status, power or wealth. Turns out we don’t respect someone simply for knowledge, seniority or even character. We respect someone for what he/she stands for. To us.

My cousin probably thinks he stands for authority. Three hundred men under him. He needed that respect. But to me, he stands for shortcuts. Whereas I stand for meaning. The pursuit of deeper meaning in life. And no way you gonna get there via shortcuts.

I realised I had to cut him off from my life. Imagine that pain. He was my big brother! I went ahead anyway. I mentally severed my ties with him. My only regret was it took me thirty years to realise this.
