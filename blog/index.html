S H A R E        

Respect.

Evaluating deep collaborative relationships.

BETWEEN AUDIENCE & filmmaker. Between filmmaker & collaborators. Between parent & child. It's the cornerstone of any relationship.

But what should you respect someone for?

Status? Power? Wealth? Any of these and the relationship will eventually fail.

How about Knowledge? Experience? Seniority?

Reverence & Respect are not the same thing.

Simply put, reverence is earned but unquestioned respect.

What about Character? Character is a huge red herring. We don't respect all good characters. Just as we don't disrespect all bad ones.

Respect someone for what he/she stands for to you. Dissociate from those you don't respect. More importantly, discover why you respect those you associate with. And you discover yourself.

Here is a detailed account on how I developed this framework.


S H A R E        

The One Word you stand for.

I stand for MEANING.

IT MAY NOT be what you're passionate about. Though it could be. What is the One Word that best represents what you want to stand for? In other words, who or what cause you aspire to become.

Now, what is the One Word for what you think of each significant person around you stands for? Compare that with your One Word. If the two words are polar opposites, the relationship will not work out.

I can't work with people who stand for SHORTCUTS. Not pithy sagely wisdom but the unethical easy way out. In life, there are no shortcuts for the pursuit of deeper meaning.

If you don't know what you stand for, you risk being defined by others according to what they choose to see of you. Knowing what you stand for attracts like-minded individuals and helps build your audience.


S H A R E        

5 Wizards.

A Holistic Approach to Illuminating Blind Spots.

DO YOU HAVE 5 people in your life you can turn to, whose perspectives you value, within 48 hours of encountering a crisis?

Are your 5 wizards wired differently enough from you, and from one another, such that their perspectives work in concert to illuminate your blind spots holistically?

Wizards don't have to be mentors or coaches. But they shouldn't be your father figure. You want a distinctive voice you trust. Not the default voice you revere. For some, their children are their wizards.

Wizarding, unlike mentoring or coaching, is a two way street. You can be a wizard to someone who's your wizard. It's not about getting advice from a sage. Not about obtaining consensus. It's about variety. Because variety induces holism.

Who you choose to be your wizards is absolutely critical. So limit those voices to 5. Most would have trouble identifying 3. View each crisis as an opportunity to reinvent yourself when you consult your wizards, and when you are someone else's wizard. The desired outcome is a lighter, enlightened mind.


S H A R E        

Fatal Flaw.

When your perceived strength limits you.

YOUR FATAL FLAW is a shortcoming that is significant because it masquerades as your strength. A quality you use to justify your success. As a result, it becomes your blind spot.

For artists, it's perfectionism. The perfection that artists seek inevitably causes them to procrastinate presenting their art to the public.

For entrepreneurs, it's optimism. An overly favorable outlook of how their product impacts their audience makes them blind to possible pitfalls.

For others, it’s diligence. The self-gratification derived from working hard makes them blind to the virtues of working smart.

For me, it's all of the above and self-righteousness. My bias towards my endeavors having deeper meaning than those of others. Sometimes, I forget that meaning is subjective.

Confirm your fatal flaw with your mentors, coaches and wizards. Have them call you out on your fatal flaw.

S H A R E        

The Problem with Self-Esteem.

When you don't know what you stand for.

BASED ON MASLOW'S Hierarchy of Needs, how do you deal with problems of Self-Esteem?

Many professionals like to believe they're not making enough money. Occasionally, I feel the same way too. Say I establish that my money issues aren't related to Safety (i.e. financial security), but to Esteem (i.e. self-esteem).

Acquiring more money will only perpetuate Esteem issues. It's an addiction that arises from a place of fear. A fear of inadequacy.

Consider reframing the problem of Self-Esteem as one of Self-Actualization.

When I determine what I Stand For, I begin to operate at the level of Self-Actualization. I stand for Meaning so I ask...

Is my current pursuit of more money meaningful? If not, does it make sense to be addicted to making money I don't need? Only to perpetuate that fear of inadequacy?

Our objective in life is to ascend the pyramid. Not descend.

WHEN YOU STRUGGLE with issues of Self-Esteem, you’re shackled by the status quo. The social norms that you subconsciously partake in perpetuating. You're measuring yourself against the standards of others.

When you struggle with issues of Self-Actualization, you’re actively questioning the status quo. Disrupting those very shackling social norms. You're measuring yourself against the standards of your own.

If you're a parent, do you want your children to be struggling with issues of Self-Esteem? Or do you want them actively pursing what they Stand For?

Don't forget your children are constantly looking up to you as their role model, wondering...

Are you Pursuing what you Stand For?

The Pro Bono Professional.

Creativity that money can't buy.

“There are wealthy gentlemen in England who drive 4 hour coaches 20-30 miles on a daily line in the summer because the privilege costs them considerable money. But if they were offered wages for the service, that would turn it into work, then they would resign.”
- Mark Twain, The Adventures of Tom Sawyer


BEHAVIOURAL ECONOMIST Dan Ariely points out that we live simultaneously in two worlds. Social norms & Market norms. Community favours vs Financial transactions.

The world you define a relationship in determines its behaviour.

Pro bono professionals are not employees or volunteers who forgo compensation for drudgery. They choose to spend time...

Exploring creative meaningful solutions for a greater purpose.

On their own terms.

Introduce money and you take that away from them. What was once play now becomes work, upending performance & creativity. Market norms enter. Social norms depart. Too little money, they feel insulted. Too much, they feel used. Both sides become calculative about their resources. The relationship is unnecessarily strained.

Meaning is the strongest way to motivate people.

Money the most expensive.

PRO BONO PROFESSIONALS get to constantly question what the project means for them and why they are in it. The autonomy to achieve competency within a community of like-minded individuals is what drives the pro bono professional.

I'm not well off but I do pro bono work when I feel aligned with the client's social cause. Because I'm not paid, to justify my time, I seek to be as involved as I'm with commissioned work. I expect the same from the client. If I sense that the client is not respecting my time or my perspective, I'm prepared to walk away.

Pro bono is not an excuse to exploit free labour.

S H A R E        

My Process.

Moving & Inspiring audiences through their hero’s journey.

WHAT MAKES A good promo video? Whether the objective is fundraising or growing awareness, my process for a promo employs feature film storytelling techniques that tug at audiences' heartstrings.

Assuming you, the client, has already established both a clearly defined objective as well as the cause (e.g. donate $45/month for child sponsorship).



1. Who is your intended audience?

No promo is successful unless it moves & inspires your audience. Not just any audience. An intended audience is an audience you define specifically with intention. Simply casting a wide net is at best, a shot in the dark.



2. Which heroes do your intended audience look up to?

Half the battle is won simply by featuring a hero (i.e. main character of the promo) who is already well-regarded by your intended audience. All the better if their hero is known to be aligned with your cause.



3. What is the hero’s own personal journey?

As a writer, I interview the hero for his/her personal journey specific to the cause. I’m seeking goals, villains, stakes and triumphs that the intended audience can identify with as part of their common struggle.

Somewhere in that mutual exploration, a unique message emerges that informs the process. Crafting a script using this approach beats any script I can possibly come up with based on pure imagination.



4. Getting the hero into character.

The hero is comprised of the person in real life and the character in the promo. In reality, the person never spends 24/7 living the life of the character.

As a director, regardless if the hero is an actor, I ensure that the hero delivers a performance on camera that is perceived as authentic to the character and not merely regurgitating lines.

This "Authentic Performance", oxymoronic as it may sound, is essential to capturing the audience’s imagination and igniting their passion. It is also the final step that culminates the creative partnership between the hero and the writer-director.

Authentic. Cathartic.

“A Hollywood blockbuster tells us the lie we want to believe. An indie film tells us the truth we don’t want to.”
 - William Goldman, Screenwriter

THE BEAUTY OF True Stories lies in the vagaries of life, where motives are driven by real-life complexities. This allows me to deliver an unadulterated perspective of the morally ambiguous world we inhabit.

That's when I realise pure fiction rarely reaches this level of truth. Though Stories are ultimately about Meaning. Truth is secondary.


S H A R E        

Are people interested in the Truth?

Stories are about Meaning. Not about Truth.

IF FACTS REPRESENT reality, Truth is a perspective of that reality, and Meaning is a selective interpretation of that perspective of that reality.

Even in writing True Stories, I occasionally fall into the trap of believing I'm telling the truth. A cold hard look reveals that I'm selectively interpreting the truth, which results in meaning.

Truth and Meaning as it turns out, are not the same thing. I can’t be telling both. The audience may think they came for the Truth. But truth be told, they want Meaning.

Because I began with the assumption that Facts represent reality, in life, the Truth is rarely as relevant as the Meaning we choose to take away from it.


S H A R E        

Church, Craft & Craving.

A Powerful Paradigm for Artists & Startups.

THE THREE VITAL elements for convincing your audience. CRAFT is the technical knowhow required to accomplish the objective. It is the How?

CRAVING is the hunger beyond passion that drives the journey. It is powered by your unique perspective based on what you stand for. It is the Why?

When an individual other than the Church anoints someone, it does not carry the same symbolic meaning. Because the Church is an authority highly regarded by Christians.

CHURCH refers to an individual or organization highly regarded by your audience that gives you split-second credibility & legitimacy when you can show their endorsement of you.


S H A R E        

Facts are meaningless.

Stories are meaningful.

HUMAN BEINGS HAVE the innate blessing, and flaw of associating subjective meaning with mere facts. Almost always involuntarily.

We convince ourselves of such meaning by believing the stories we tell ourselves. Thankfully, this is why moving and inspiring films have the ability to transform an audience.

As story consultant on the space endeavour, the Facts are a man in a box rises to the sky before falling back to Earth. The Story we tell ourselves is a Singaporean goes to Space.

We built a following because 'Singapore' obviously meant something to our audience. But that story didn't land for other Singaporeans. Perhaps they weren't our target audience. Perhaps it didn't mean as much to them. So we ask ourselves...

How can we tell this story more meaningfully?

In Asia & Africa, a self-imposed discrimination aka “Colonial Disease” manifests itself in the local government favouring the White Man.

In Singapore, Malay & Indian minorities struggle to conform under a certain "Chinese privilege" conferred to the Chinese majority, a variant of Sinocentrism experienced in China.

Intersectionality is the Interconnected Nature of Race, Class & Gender that creates overlapping & interdependent systems of Discrimination or Disadvantage.
