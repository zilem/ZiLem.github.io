Respect.

A Framework for Evaluating Deep Collaborative Relationships.

Between audience & filmmaker. Between filmmaker & collaborators. Between parent & child. It's the cornerstone of any relationship.

But what should you respect someone for?

Character? Knowledge? Seniority? Status? Power? Wealth? Any of these and the relationship will eventually fail. Especially character. Character is a huge red herring. We don't respect all good characters, just as we don't disrespect all bad characters.

Respect someone for what he/she stands for to you. Dissociate from those you don't respect. More importantly, discover why you respect those you associate with. And you discover yourself.

What's the
"One Word"
you stand for?

I stand for MEANING.

Now, ask those around you to think of one word you stand for. If they respect you, their "One Word" of you would be what they respect of you.

Compare your "One Word" for what you stand for with your "One Word" for what they stand for. If the two words are polar opposites, the relationship will not work out.

I can't work with people who stand for SHORTCUTS. In life, there is no shortcut for the pursuit of deeper meaning. Knowing what you stand for helps define your audience.

Church, Craft & Craving

A Powerful Paradigm for Artists & Startups.

The 3 vital elements for convincing your audience. CRAFT is the technical knowhow required to accomplish the objective. CRAVING is the hunger beyond passion that drives the journey. What about CHURCH?

When an individual other than the Church anoints/baptises someone, it does not carry the same symbolic meaning. Why? Because the Church is an authority highly regarded by Christians.

CHURCH refers to an individual or organization highly regarded by your audience that gives you credibility & legitimacy when you associate yourself with them.
