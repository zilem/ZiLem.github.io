Thinking along the z-Axis.

Out-of-the-plane thinking.

Most experiences in life lead us to believe that the solution to a problem is often binary. Either this or that. As if the world exists on a plane.

What if we could elevate ourselves above the plane? How would the world appear to us? Here is a collection of revelations, values and beliefs that have shaped my path as an artist.


S H A R E            

Respect.

Evaluating deep collaborative relationships.

Between audience & filmmaker. Between filmmaker & collaborators. Between parent & child. It's the cornerstone of any relationship.

But what should you respect someone for?

Character? Knowledge? Seniority? Status? Power? Wealth? Any of these and the relationship will eventually fail. Especially character. Character is a huge red herring. We don't respect all good characters, just as we don't disrespect all bad characters.

Respect someone for what he/she stands for to you. Dissociate from those you don't respect. More importantly, discover why you respect those you associate with. And you discover yourself.


S H A R E            

What's the
"One Word"
you stand for?

I stand for MEANING.

Now, ask those around you to think of one word you stand for. If they respect you, their "One Word" of you would be what they respect of you.

Compare your "One Word" for what you stand for with your "One Word" for what they stand for. If the two words are polar opposites, the relationship will not work out.

I can't work with people who stand for SHORTCUTS. In life, there are no shortcuts for the pursuit of deeper meaning.

If you don't know what you stand for, you risk letting others define what you stand for. Knowing what you stand for helps you define your audience.

S H A R E            

Church, Craft & Craving.

A Powerful Paradigm for Artists & Startups.

The 3 vital elements for convincing your audience. CRAFT is the technical knowhow required to accomplish the objective. CRAVING is the hunger beyond passion that drives the journey. What about CHURCH?

When an individual other than the Church anoints someone, it does not carry the same symbolic meaning. Why? Because the Church is an authority highly regarded by Christians.

CHURCH refers to an individual or organization highly regarded by your audience that gives you split-second credibility & legitimacy when you can show their endorsement of you.

S H A R E            

Intersectionality.

In America, the gender discrimination faced by a White Female and the racial discrimination faced by an Asian Male both stem from a toxic white masculine culture.

In Asia & Africa, a self-imposed discrimination aka “Colonial Disease” manifests itself in the local government favouring the White Man.

In multiracial Singapore, Malay & Indian minorities struggle to conform under a certain "Chinese privilege" conferred to the Chinese majority, a variant of Sinocentrism experienced in China.

Intersectionality is the Interconnected Nature of Race, Class & Gender that creates overlapping & interdependent systems of Discrimination or Disadvantage.

What's the point of Language?

For most people, it's communication with others. But is that all? Who's the person you spend most of the time communicating with?

Yourself!

When you have certain hunches or gut feelings that can't be expressed in words, are you guilty of being a lazy thinker? Don't you desire to understand yourself better? Reflection begins with asking yourself "Why?"

Language as a tool for thought.

For all that Singapore prides itself on innovation, is it destined to become a 'smart nation' of landlords, materialists & conformists? Pragmatism is the modern day shackles we must break free of. We need role models who champion ideals & autonomy.
