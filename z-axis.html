z-Axis

Pithy Ideas To Go.

Here is a collection of ideas, values & beliefs that have shaped my path as an artist. If conformity is a plane, these are thoughts along the z-Axis.

Respect.

Between audience & filmmaker. Between filmmaker & collaborators. Even between parent & child. It's the cornerstone of any relationship.

Respect someone for what he stands for. His core values. Not for his seniority, status, power or wealth. You can't. If you try, the relationship will eventualy fall apart. Not even love, family or friendship can keep it together.

Authentic. Cathartic.

“A Hollywood blockbuster tells us the lie we want to believe. An indie film tells us the truth we don’t want to.”
- William Goldman, Screenwriter

The beauty of True Stories lies in the vagaries of life, where motives are driven by real-life complexities. This allows me to deliver an unadulterated perspective of the morally ambiguous world we inhabit.

Facts are meaningless.

Human beings have the innate ability to associate any meaning to mere facts. Involuntarily. We convince ourselves of such meaning by believing the stories we tell ourselves. Simply because...

Stories are meaningful.

Intersectionality.

In America, the gender discrimination faced by a White Female and the racial discrimination faced by an Asian Male both stem from a toxic white masculine culture.

In Asia & Africa, a self-imposed discrimination aka “Colonial Disease” manifests itself in the local government favouring the White Man.

In multiracial Singapore, Malay & Indian minorities struggle to conform under a certain "Chinese privilege" conferred to the Chinese majority, a variant of Sinocentrism experienced in China.

Intersectionality is the Interconnected Nature of Race, Class & Gender that creates overlapping & interdependent systems of Discrimination or Disadvantage.

One who claims certain hunches or feelings can't be expressed in words maybe guilty of being a lazy thinker. Literally for that moment.

Language has a dual function. In Singapore, critical thinking is not practised because language is seen primarily as a means of communication with others. More important is communicating with the self. Which begins with asking oneself why.

Language can be a Powerful tool for Thought.

Pragmatism as Oppression.

"The most potent weapon of the oppressor is the mind of the oppressed."
- Steven Biko, Anti-Apartheid Activist

What happens when those with the power to say no, lack the balls to say yes to true innovation?

For all that Singapore prides itself on innovation, is it destined to become a 'smart nation' of landlords, materialists & conformists? Pragmatism is the modern day shackles we must break free of. We need role models who champion ideals & autonomy.
