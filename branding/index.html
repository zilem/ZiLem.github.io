BRANDING

Giving your Audience a compelling Identity they can't resist.

I BELIEVE THERE are two components to branding and a third that makes it extremely successful.

1.

Defining your brand's authentic identity.


2.

Addressing why your intended audience needs your product.

Most agencies reverse engineer an incomplete understanding of your brand identity based on a vague idea of your target audience. This is unsustainable in the long run due to a lack of self-awareness.

Who am I?

My forte lies in helping others understand what makes them tick. I distill the essence of what defines you from casual conversation. Knowing what defines you is paramount because a brand based on your core principles & values would operate seamlessly with who you are as a person.


Branding is defining your brand's authentic identity in a way that addresses why your intended audience needs your product.

My definition of branding is unconcerned with having a USP (Unique Selling Proposition).


Being unique is the outcome.
It should never be the objective.

An intended audience is not the same as a target audience. The latter suggests a particular group you’re aiming to market to. The former is a group the brand/founder defines with intention. This begins with asking why your brand exists. Its raison d’être. Not what your brand is selling.

Your brand’s reason for existence should speak to your own insecurities on some level. Because this allows you to understand the insecurities of your intended audience. This is profound. Because now you know why they need your product. Not just what product they are looking for.


Branding is futile when a client doesn’t have a strong enough why because they are contented with selling a commonplace what.


DEVELOPING A SUCCESSFUL brand is all about storytelling. Of all the storytelling professions, the screenwriter is the only storyteller whose story forms the blueprint of a multimillion-dollar product designed to entertain its audience, appeal to human emotions and even promote a cause.

As a screenwriter-filmmaker, I apply these techniques to branding. We begin with a conversation about your personal journey specific to your brand. I’m seeking goals, villains, stakes and triumphs that your intended audience can identify with as part of their common struggle.

Somewhere in that mutual exploration, we may discover that your brand speaks to a higher cause.


In serving the higher cause,
a brand transcends itself to become a movement.

The third component that makes a brand extremely successful is when your movement is so compelling that your intended audience can’t resist deriving their own identity from your cause. Think Apple and Virgin.


Past the tipping point, your brand becomes legend.
